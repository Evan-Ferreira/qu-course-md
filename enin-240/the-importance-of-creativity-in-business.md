# The Importance of Creativity in Business

**Source:** HBS Online — [online.hbs.edu/blog/post/importance-of-creativity-in-business](https://online.hbs.edu/blog/post/importance-of-creativity-in-business)
**Author:** Michael Boyles | **Published:** January 25, 2022

---

Creativity and innovation matter across all industries — not just traditionally "creative" roles — because every business faces challenges that demand inventive solutions. The article covers why creativity matters, how it connects to design thinking, and practical ways to foster it.

## Why Is Creativity Important?

The article identifies five key reasons creativity matters in business:

1. **It Accompanies Innovation** — Innovation requires novelty _and_ usefulness. Creative thinking generates unique ideas, which form the raw material for truly innovative solutions.

2. **It Increases Productivity** — Creativity enables working smarter rather than harder. While routine and structure are valuable, they shouldn't come at the cost of improvement. A creative environment can significantly boost productivity.

3. **It Allows for Adaptability** — Internal and external disruptions (like the COVID-19 pandemic) require imaginative responses to maintain operations. Creative problem-solving doesn't always mean overhauling the business model — small adjustments can solve big problems.

4. **It's Necessary for Growth** — Cognitive fixedness — the belief that there's only one way to approach a situation — is a major barrier to growth. Leaders who fail to encourage creative thinking risk stagnation.

5. **It's an In-Demand Skill** — Industries like healthcare and manufacturing actively seek creativity and innovation skills. Design thinking and creative problem-solving help job seekers differentiate themselves.

## Creativity and Design Thinking

Design thinking provides concrete structure for the abstract creative process. HBS Dean Srikant Datar's framework breaks it into four iterative stages:

- **Clarify** — Use observation and empathy (both data-driven and intuitive) to understand the situation and the people affected.
- **Ideate** — Brainstorm freely and generate creative solutions without constraint.
- **Develop** — Experiment, prototype, and critique — turning abstract ideas into testable solutions through trial and error.
- **Implement** — Deploy the solution, communicating its value and overcoming existing biases.

### The Operational World vs. The Innovation World

The article distinguishes two sides of business:

- The **operational world** is concrete and structured — focused on metrics, routine, and results. It provides analytical tools but tends to undervalue creativity and punish risky failures.
- The **innovation world** thrives on curiosity, speculation, and experimentation. It drives growth but lacks the structure for day-to-day efficiency.

Both are essential, and leaders must navigate between them. Design thinking bridges this gap by moving between concrete and abstract stages.

## How to Encourage Creativity and Innovation

Seven practical tips for fostering a creative workplace:

1. **Don't Be Afraid to Take Risks** — Stepping outside comfort zones is essential to innovation and growth.
2. **Don't Punish Failure** — Many great innovations emerged from repeated failures. Treat setbacks as learning opportunities.
3. **Provide the Resources Necessary to Innovate** — Invest in tools, training, and funding so teams can actually act on creative ideas.
4. **Don't Try to Measure Results Too Quickly** — Some ideas take time to yield results. Avoid discarding promising innovations prematurely.
5. **Maintain an Open Mind** — Continuously challenge your biases and stay receptive to unexpected ideas, even incomplete ones.
6. **Foster Collaboration** — Teamwork toward a shared goal accelerates innovation. Ensure everyone has a voice through brainstorming sessions.
7. **Encourage Diversity** — Diverse teams combat groupthink. Bringing together people from different backgrounds and experiences sparks more creative thinking.

## Learning to Be Creative in Business

The article recommends engaging with peers who practice design thinking, exploring real-world scenarios, and considering formal education (such as HBS Online's _Design Thinking and Innovation_ course) to develop these skills.
